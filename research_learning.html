ICT Research & Experiments

Structured guidance for machine learning, CNNs, data pipelines, and publishing.

Research Process

  1. Topic Selection: Identify gap, survey literature, refine research questions.
  2. Hypothesis & Objectives: Define clear aims and testable hypotheses.
  3. Methodology: Choose experimental designs (e.g., CNN architecture, datasets).
  4. Implementation: Set up data pipelines, code reproducible experiments.
  5. Evaluation: Metrics (accuracy, precision, recall), statistical validation.
  6. Conclusion & Future Work: Summarize findings, propose next steps.

Tools & Frameworks

Python & Libraries

NumPy, pandas, scikit-learn for data preprocessing; Matplotlib for visualization.

Deep Learning

TensorFlow, PyTorch for building and training CNNs; Keras for rapid prototyping.

Experiment Tracking

Weights & Biases, MLflow for logging experiments, hyperparameters, and model versions.

Data Management

  • Collection: Public datasets (ImageNet, CIFAR), web scraping, APIs.
  • Preprocessing: Cleaning, normalization, augmentation techniques for images and signals.
  • Storage: Use versioned data repositories (DVC) and cloud storage (AWS S3).

Writing & Publishing

  1. Manuscript Structure: Abstract, Introduction, Methods, Results, Discussion, References.
  2. Reference Management: Zotero, Mendeley for citations and bibliography.
  3. Target Venues: IEEE, ACM conferences/journals relevant to ICT and ML.
  4. Peer Review: Preprint servers (arXiv), gather feedback from peers before submission.
